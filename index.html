About Me

Hello! I'm Yu-Chen Liao. I'm honing my expertise with a Master's in Computational Science and Engineering at Georgia Tech. My foundational Industrial Engineering and Management education was acquired at the National Yang-Ming Chiao Tung University in Taiwan. I enjoyed two immersive internships at Garmin that significantly shaped my professional journey. Initially, I served as a Software Engineer Intern. Here, I engaged with various programs, from tools that kept track of activated cards on devices to software that effortlessly converted Word document tables into .txt files. My subsequent role was as a Software Quality Assurance Engineer. Tasked with GPS-related functionalities, I not only deepened my understanding of GPS principles but also developed a unique tool in C++ that transformed Cartesian coordinates into the Terrestrial Coordinate System. This initiative dramatically reduced calculation times for my team. One of my standout college projects was the THSR booking system, a user-friendly platform for train ticket reservations. My ambition at Georgia Tech is crystal clear: to sharpen my technical prowess and position myself at the forefront of tech innovation. Collaboration, straightforward communication, and embracing novel ideas are paramount in tech. Thank you for visiting my space! I'm eager to connect and explore potential collaborations.
